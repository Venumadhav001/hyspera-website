The Architecture

Three Systems. One Conviction.

01

Type IV COPV Hydrogen Tank

High pressure storage at 700 bar. Carbon fibre overwound over a polymer liner. Significantly lighter than metal alternatives. Validated for structural integrity under cyclic fill and thermal variation.

02

PEM Fuel Cell Powertrain

Proton exchange membrane technology. High continuous power output. Efficiency well above internal combustion engines. Near-silent operation. The only output is water vapour.

03

Intelligent Energy Management

Real-time power split between the fuel cell and a buffer battery. State of charge kept within an optimal window. Built around Indian drive cycles and long-distance highway conditions.

Powertrain and Performance

Engineered Without Compromise.

HySPERA is building a full hydrogen fuel cell passenger car from the ground up. Not a conversion. Not a concept. A purpose-built vehicle designed around the strengths of hydrogen as an energy carrier.

The powertrain centres on a PEM fuel cell paired with a lithium-ion buffer battery. The fuel cell handles steady-state demand. The battery covers transient peaks and recovers energy from braking. The two work together through a real-time energy management system.

The hydrogen storage system uses a Type IV COPV rated to 700 bar. Rear-wheel drive. A permanent magnet synchronous motor delivers torque directly to the wheels with no gearbox needed.

Target range is above 600 km on a single fill. Refuel time is 3 to 5 minutes. Zero tailpipe emissions.

Full technical specifications will be published at a later stage. HySPERA is currently in the research and simulation phase.

About HySPERA

A Founder's Conviction.


HySPERA is building India's first premium hydrogen fuel cell passenger car. The company was started by a researcher who believes hydrogen is not a distant promise. It is the right answer for long-distance mobility in a country that cannot afford range anxiety or overnight charging infrastructure.

India's geography demands real range. The average intercity distance, the lack of charging density outside major urban centres, and the heat and humidity that degrades battery performance all point to the same conclusion. Hydrogen, with a short refuel time and a long range, fits the problem.

HySPERA is not a component supplier. It is not a consultancy. It is building the full vehicle. From tank to powertrain to cabin.
