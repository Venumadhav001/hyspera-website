The Architecture

Three Systems.
One Conviction.

Every component chosen because it is right for the problem.

01
Type IV COPV Hydrogen Tank
Rated to 700 bar. Carbon fibre overwound over a high-density polymer liner. Significantly lighter than metal tank equivalents. Simulation-validated structural integrity under cyclic fill and thermal variation across operating conditions.
→
02
PEM Fuel Cell Powertrain
Proton exchange membrane technology. Hydrogen enters at the anode and splits into protons and electrons. Electrons generate electricity through an external circuit. The only output at the cathode is water vapour. No combustion at any stage.
→
03
Intelligent Energy Management
Real-time power split between the fuel cell and a lithium-ion buffer battery. State of charge is maintained within a controlled operating window to protect battery longevity and ensure regenerative braking capacity is always available.
→

HySPERA's powertrain is built around a proton exchange membrane fuel cell delivering 110 kW continuous output, paired with a 2.3 kWh lithium-ion buffer battery. The battery handles transient load demands and recuperates braking energy while the fuel cell maintains steady-state output.

The hydrogen storage system uses a single Type IV carbon fibre overwound pressure vessel, rated to 700 bar, storing 7.5 kg of hydrogen in 150 litres. Simulation-validated for structural integrity across cyclic fill conditions and a wide thermal range.

A permanent magnet synchronous motor delivering 100 kW and 300 Nm of torque drives the rear wheels. Full torque from zero speed. No gearbox required.

Refuelled in 3 to 5 minutes. Target range of 600 km and above. Near-silent. Zero tailpipe emissions.

About HySPERA

A Founder's
Conviction.

HySPERA is building India's first premium hydrogen fuel cell passenger car. Founded by a researcher who believes hydrogen is not a distant promise. It is the right answer for long-distance mobility in a country that cannot afford range anxiety or overnight charging.

We are not a component company. We are not a concept studio. We are building the full vehicle. From tank to powertrain to cabin.

India's geography demands range. India's infrastructure cannot wait for overnight charging networks to cover 3.3 million square kilometres. Hydrogen, with its fast refuel and long range, is the answer that fits the problem.

Currently in founding stage. R&D in Germany. Manufacturing target: India. Not seeking public investment at this time.
