The Architecture

Three Systems. One Conviction.

01

Type IV COPV Hydrogen Tank

700 bar rated. Carbon fibre overwound over a high-density polymer liner. Up to 70% lighter than metal tanks. Simulation-validated structural integrity under cyclic fill and thermal variation.

02

PEM Fuel Cell Powertrain

Proton exchange membrane. 110 kW continuous output. 50–60% efficiency — significantly higher than any combustion engine. Near-silent operation. Zero tailpipe emissions — water vapour only.

03

Intelligent Energy Management

Real-time power split between fuel cell and 2.3 kWh buffer battery. SOC maintained between 20–80%. Optimised for Indian drive cycles and long-distance highway demands.

HySPERA's powertrain centres on a proton exchange membrane fuel cell delivering 110 kW continuous output — paired with a 2.3 kWh lithium-ion buffer battery that handles transient load demands and recuperates braking energy.

The hydrogen storage system uses a Type IV carbon fibre overwound pressure vessel, rated to 700 bar and simulation-validated for structural integrity under cyclic fill and thermal variation.

A PMSM motor converts the electrical output into 100 kW and 300 Nm of mechanical torque at the rear wheels — with full torque available from zero speed and no gearbox required.

Refuelled in 3 to 5 minutes. Range of 550 to 650 km. Near-silent. Zero tailpipe emissions.

About HySPERA

A Founder's Conviction.


HySPERA is building India's first premium hydrogen fuel cell passenger car. Founded by a researcher who believes hydrogen is not a distant promise — it is the right answer for long-distance mobility in a country that cannot afford range anxiety or overnight charging.

We are not a component company. We are not a concept studio. We are building the full vehicle. From tank to powertrain to cabin.

India's geography demands range. India's infrastructure cannot wait for overnight charging networks to cover 3.3 million square kilometres. Hydrogen, with its 3-to-5-minute refuel and 600 km range, is the answer that fits the problem.
